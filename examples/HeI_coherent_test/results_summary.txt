Sanity checks (all PASS):
  1. N_scat agrees between inc and coh to < 0.1 % at every (source, tau).
     Confirms the upper-state selection probability is unchanged, as the
     cross-section is the incoherent 1:3:5 Lorentzian sum (J'-J' interference
     cancels in W11 + 2*W14).

  2. sum(Jout) is identical between inc and coh.
     Confirms photon-number conservation.

  3. tau=0.1 cases: N_scat ~ 0.1 means most photons escape without scattering
     even once -- the spectrum is dominated by the source profile and the
     coherent treatment effectively has no signature here.

  4. Image-integrated p (sum_pixels Q,U / sum I) ~ 0 for all runs --
     correct for axisymmetric sphere viewed from infinity by a single
     observer (azimuthal cancellation of Q,U).

  5. Per-pixel polarization p(b) is highest for the optically thin point-source
     case (~12 % at b ~ 0.8 for tau=1) and decreases with tau as multiple
     scatterings depolarize.

  6. Coherent vs. incoherent differences in p(b):
     - tau=0.1 / 1 / 10: difference below the Monte Carlo noise (single/few
       scatterings sample the line core where coherent and incoherent E1
       nearly coincide).
     - tau=1000: p(b) changes by 5-15% (relative); the coherent treatment
       slightly increases p because the modified E1(nu) keeps photons more
       linearly polarized in the wings where they spend more time at high tau.
